Content and media strategy of The Croatian National Tourist Board
  • Client:  The Croatian National Tourist Board (CNTB), Croatia’s national tourist organisation
  • Brief:  Currently, CNTB is focused on increasing young Chinese people visiting to Croatia and having overnight stays. However, due to lack of a clear content and media strategy and knowledge regarding the Chinese market and Chinese audiences, CNTB is struggling to reach them via social media platforms.

    As a result, an external, internal analysis and organisation strategy were analysed to outline the content and media function to find out the gaps between the media function, organisational strategy and target audience. Second, a persona analysis was conducted among the target audience to gather more in depth information about the persona. Then a content creation and a distribution plan was provided to promote CNTB’s content and reach the audience
  • My role:  Content Researcher
  • Process:  Decoding CNTB’s organisational strategy and situation (external, internal analysis) to find out the gaps between audience and company - conducting persona analysis among young Chinese people (qualitative and quantitative research) - content creation (content treatment) - distribution plan (implementing on platforms) - AB test
    • Goals:

    The original goal is to stimulate the number of Chinese people to pay a visit to Croatia but after having a fully understanding of CNTB’s organisational strategy and situation; the goal was redefined to “raise awareness of Croatia as a tourism destination among young Chinese people with content and media strategy”. Additionally, before reaching the goal, there were serval problems needed to be solved.

    The problems are below:
    1. Lacking precise knowledge about the Chinese audience, CNTB is unable to distribute the correct content to reach their target audience
    2. Low brand awareness among young Chinese people, many Chinese audiences have no clue where Croatia is
    3. No specific content and media strategy, CNTB has not developed a structure to explain and communicate with their audiences

    Personas:

    To solve the first problem, a research-based description of CNTB’s customer persona and a customer journey map through the stages of the awareness and consideration process were created. Via that, the first problem was answered.

    CNTB’s focus on attract individual travellers’ attention; this group includes office workers and students, they are good at English and finding out the travel information via the internet. Most of them using the Internet to search information, and it is easily to approach than via social networking. For specific information, see below picture:

    Content Treatment:

    To raise brand awareness among Zihan and develop a strategy to communicate with her, an authentic and personalised content framework were created and utilized to helps CNTB catch up Zihan’s attention. According to Zihan, she likes using articles and videos from micro-influencers to find information related to travel. Owing to that, CNTB will cooperate with micro-influencers to create content in order to enhance the Zihan’s awareness.

    Zihan also makes use of Xiaohongshu, Weibo and online-travel platforms to find information related to travel. Those information offers inspirations for her being aware of Croatian tourism and further searches information related to Croatia travel. Then, long-reads will continuously give an online in-depth tourism experience for her.

    Finally, a distribution plan followed the *SOSTAC was also utilised to plan the promotion and delivery of the content. Through those methods the goal will be completed. To be specific, several official social media accounts will be created for CNTB to post content frequently on Xiaohongshu and Weibo to have connection and communication with Zihan. The content will pay attention to Zihan's preferred topics (in relation to the content of the Long-Read) to continuously inspire Zihan's interests and engagement to encourage Zihan visited Croatia.

    SOSTAC: Situation Analysis, Objectives, Strategy, Tactics, Actions, Control
    Limitation & Reflection

    Due to geographic range limits, it is hard to reach mainland Chinese people from first-tier cities (Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Shenzhen) via social media. The researcher has intended to connect people and friends who have families, friends from Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Shenzhen to send out the questionnaire and had interviewed with them, but the researcher could only reach the Chinese people from Hong Kong, Macao, Changsha, Shenyang, Harbin, Quanzhou, Xiamen and so on. Consumers from different regions and cities have vastly different income levels, behaviours, and trends. If the researcher conducted research on young Chinese people who live in first-tier cities, the outcome of persona section and content treatment might be different. Since people from first-tier cities, the disposable income are higher than other second-tier and third-tier cities. They might prefer flocking to Outlet Malls Abroad rather than go to museums or historical sites.

    Anota - Text-Based AI in Healthcare

    ×
    Anota - Text-Based AI in Healthcare
  • Client:  National Health Insurance Administration (National Health Insurance Administration of Taiwan)
  • Brief: NHIA is working on labelling medical data, extract and analyse health data fast and accurately. Nevertheless, most of the NHIA’s project managers had no experience in managing projects and the annotators did not equip with sufficient knowledge towards health care. That led to the quality of data was too low to be systematised and the annotators also had to spend plenty of time on manually label the data.

    Owing to that, Anota was designed to solve those problems. Anota is not only can help the users to extract, find, and analyse data from a variety of medical document types quickly and accurately, but also can help the users to manage a project from start to finish effectively, classify medical documents and using AI model to improve annotation quality.
  • My role:  UX experience design, UI design
  • Process:discussed the function with the product manager and engineers - based on the persona’s needs and pain-points to create wireframes & flowchart - prototype testing - UI design
  • Goals: help the annotators and the project managers run the project smoothly and effectively
    • Problems:

    After several meetings and interviews with the users, the problems were defined more specific and detailed, here are the problems:

    1. NHIA did not provide completed new employee orientation for the annotators and most of them do not have background knowledge of health care; they constantly felt confused on the task, but there was no one can guide them, so they had to take the task longer than PM expected.

    2. PMs always felt confusion or forgot about the tasks were assigned to who and needed accurate time tracking that alerts when tasks go over time to prevent bottlenecks.

    Persona

    The users were divided into "Project Producer, Annotator”, "Project Manager”, and “Supervisors”; those three kind of personas to look at their usage behaviours in making medical projects, annotating medical documents, assess medical documents to evaluating their skills, concerns, goals, and technology in relation to develop important insights about features and functionality in this software.

    Flowchart & Wireframe:

    Having the interviews with users, the result revealed that there are knowledge gap, intention-behaviour gap (intention to use and actual usage behaviour) between three kinds of users that leaded to main problems: the low quality of health documents. To knock down those gaps, I designed the wireframes and flowchart based on personas’ needs and pain-points to design a management process which help the PMs assign tasks to the right people, and a notification to alert that the tasks might go over time. For the annotators, a fully guideline was designed to explain medical vocabulary to help them understand what they are doing now and what to do in next step.

    Typography

    To be sure that the users can read the text without effort and make good use of the information we want to convey. Therefore, the most crucial requirement for fonts is that it is legible. Japanese sans-serif was applied in Chinese, and for English, sans-serif was applied because of its readability and legibility in web and mobile user interfaces.

    Color:

    Color blue has already used so often by healthcare providers and other related organisations, users already associate it with the field of medicine. Besides, color blue is typically associated with credibility, trust, knowledge, power, professionalism, cleanliness, calm and focus and all of these qualities are valued in the medical community.

    Design Library:

    This software aims to help the users to mange the annotation project and make annotation process quickly. Owing to that, it is critical to design the UI as simple as we can to avoid the UI complexity for the users; the users will feel nervous and user churn is going to be high. The UI style pays attention on designing a simple UI. Additionally, there are many layout pages in Anota; it is necessary to build a design system as a collection of repeatable, reusable components and guidelines that explains how to use each of those components. By doing so, it is not only speeding up the design process, eliminating inconsistency in design, and minimising design entropy to the development team, but also building a better user experience and interface for the users.

    Limitation & Reflection

    The contract with NHIA points out that we are only allow to have connected with a few of the users (Project Manager) to do interviews and testings. Most of the insight from annotators were from their supervisors. That is, without getting the whole vision of the complete scenario during the users facing precise problems, challenging some assumption, the feedback might remain error.

    Drug pricing search platform

    ×
    Drug pricing search platform
  • Client:  National Health Insurance Administration
  • Brief:  to regulate drug prices and quality, the users have to access other counties’ drug price website to compare the price, repeatedly and collect needed drug price as a report, then showing the report to pharmaceutical sales representative in order to negotiate the drug price. This is not only wasting a bunch of time, but also delaying the users’ important work. For the sake of time management, this platform was developed. The users merely need to use this platform to search drug prices of other counties and it will show up the historic, current drug price and so on. This platform applied web crawler to automatically access ten countries’ drug price websites to obtain data via a software program. By doing so, the users are able to get more work done with less time and resources and it is also save time for solving important problems.
  • My role:  UX experience design, UI design
  • Process:  defined the persona’s needs and pain-points – discussed the function with the product manager and engineers – created flowchart and wireframes – UI design –  tested the product – released
  • Problems:  the users are unable to speed up finding out the medical data.
  • Goal:  help the user collecting accurate and relevant data and execute work successfully
    • Persona

    Responsibility: negotiates drug price with pharmaceutical sales representative

    Necessary Skills: basic knowledge about ingredients in medicines, understand basic name of medicine in English, but Chinese is still intuition.

    Scenarios: search drug prices in different countries’ drug prices website including Japan, Germany, and Sweden. Therefore, he has to translate the language to Chinese frequently. It is also troublesome for him to see different tabs on browser. He has to put all of the data together, then export pdf file to convey detailed statistics about ingredients in medicines with supervisors and negotiates drug prices with Pharmaceutical Sales Representative.

    After getting a fully understanding of the persona’s needs and pain-points, creating flow chart to represent the process of searching drug price with this platform. Then wireframes were created to depict the page layout of the platform's content, including interface elements and navigational systems, and how they work together. Finally, a prototype were used to discuss the process of this platform with the users.

    Design process

    First, to display a large amount of numerical data and lists of drug related information, I decided to take the benefits of a table UI design. Secondly, to make the platform available in 10 different languages, Google Translate was installed to translate the language, immediately. Finally, the persona has mentioned that he was sick to copy the related data together and export the file. He hopes that the data will be placed in one page, which could be printed a copy or exported as a pdf file. So the function of pdf and print were embedded to solve this problem.

    Typography

    In terms of Typography in Chinese, concerning this platform is on web/mobile. The read is quit important in this part, so Japanese sans-serif was applied.

    Visual & Colors

    This platform was used to search drug price, so color green was applied to associations with relaxing and calming. Besides, to make the users can easily understand and navigate the interface. Different colors were utilised to distinguish the data in a grid.
